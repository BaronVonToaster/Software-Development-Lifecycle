# Software-Development-Lifecycle
This project is the culmination of my Software Development Lifecycle course. This project helped in my preparation to plan and implement the Agile methodology. In this course, I was able to take on the role of each member of the  Scrum Team to plan, define, and adapt our customer's product.

1. How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

The product is known as a solution, because it has a conjuctive problem it must solve. Understanding the problem is essential to developing the solution. Those who will benefit the most are those who most consistently deal with the problem. User stories keep the needs in the purview of the users themselves for the team. 

2. How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

As I continue implementing Agile into my current and future projects, I will be taking advantage of the concepts of backlog refinement, pair programming, and story estimation tools like story point poker. I intend on being very intentional with the structure of my future projects by utilizing the artifacts, events, and roles where possible. 

3. What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
   
In the world of software devlopment, to be a good team member, it takes humility, dedication, and collaboration. The ability to recognize that I am not the team but a block in the structure of a greater organization. This is relevant from enterprise organizations down to a two-person startup. Seeing projects through to the end ensures that the team reaches their intended goals, even when the path to it take unintended detours. Lastly, the ability to seek and give assistance will lead to a cohesive team in contrast to siloed individuals.
